Washington (July 2023) Hike Log

When I hike, I like to take a pen and small moleskin to collect my thoughts and feelings each night. This 6 day hike of a section of the Pacific Crest Trail (PCT) was a wellspring of ideas and perspectives. This log is a short synopsis of a few of those thoughts.

Day 0: Travel Day! (Ginette Lake)

Originally, this was to be Day 2 of our journey into the Pacific Northwest. However, my 5:48pm flight (on July 15th) was delayed to 7:48pm and then further delayed, due to mechanical failures. (This reminds me of my last hike to Colorado which also had delays.) Unlike my last trip, this one was ultimately canceled for the next morning. So, after a few more delays, we were finally off for Seattle.

My hiking partners (trail names: “Bucket” and “Geo”) arrived yesterday as planned. Our original plan was to drive from our hotel at 6:30am to the ending point of our 65 mile hike at the White Pass trailhead. At 11am, we were scheduled to meet a (shuttle) driver that we hired to take us and our gear to our starting location, Trout Lake trailhead, which would have been a 3 hour drive. Alas, my flight cancellation nixed that idea.

Luckily, our hike leader (“Bucket”) was considering options and opted for an “out and back” hike: start out at White Pass and hike as far as we can, then return. When I asked about whether we would be able to hike the Knife’s Edge section (considered the highlight by many PCT hikers), he noted:

Oh yeah - we’ll do it twice. Less of a warm up and straight into the highlight reel. We’ll go south from White Pass (over Knife’s Edge on day 2, maybe as far as Walupt Lake), and then we’ll return the same way heading north. We’ll have Mt. Adams as our beacon on first leg, and Mt. Rainier as our beacon on return leg. It will not suck!!!

During my flight in, I contemplated this hiking trip. I had high hopes for this trip. I tried my best to prepare: hiking the W/OD trail with my backpack loaded up with 45 pounds of barbells, meticulously recording and weighing each piece of gear, preparing my clothes with permethrin. I even taste-tested all of my dehydrated meals. I thought, “all that remains is execution.” That, and taking each moment to soak in the sights, smells and sounds of nature.

I’m amazed at the magnitude of these volcanos from the plane.

Mt. Rainier? (or is it Mt. Hood?) from my aisle seat.

After yet another delay at the airport, we finally make our way to the trail head. By 5:07pm, we have arrived and I quickly change out of my street clothes into my hiking gear. One quick picture at the trailhead and we’re off!

Me, Geo and Bucket at the White Pass trailhead

Given the lateness of our start, we will only get a few miles on our trail before we set up camp at Ginette Lake and share a ceremonial beer.

Me and Bucket celebrate our first campsite with a Florida Man beer

I first met Bucket in college at Case Western Reserve University (Cleveland, OH). He was a year ahead of me. Though we were both among the same friends, we weren’t very close. This hiking experience (some 36 years after our time in college) was our chance to really get to know each other. Our renewed friendship has been one of the biggest wins from this trip. I plan to do more hikes with Bucket!

Geo has been friends with Bucket for many years, meeting soon after Bucket left CWRU with his master's degree. I really enjoyed getting to know him as well.

It takes some work, but I’m glad to be here!

–Geo

Getting out of your comfort zone does take work. We plan to wake at 4:45am to break camp for our first full day of hiking!
